Exploring Massachusetts Opportunity Zones

Overview of Program

The Opportunity Zone Program was created as part of the U.S. Tax Cut and Jobs Act of 2017 to incentivize investment in low income communities. State governors can nominate up to 25% of their stateâ€™s low income census tracts to be deemed Opportunity Zones. Capital gains from investments in Opportunity Zones are given favorable tax treatment through the program. The program presents an opportunity for both Opportunity Zone residents and private investors to benefit from investments.

In Massachusetts, the U.S. Treasury has designated 138 census tracts in 79 communities as Opportunity Zones. The map below displays the 138 Opportunity Zones in Massachusetts. Interact with the map to view summary statistics on each community.

Considerations of Opportunity Zones

Opportunity Zones can be a powerful tool to attract investment into often overlooked communities. However, national and local critics question the certification of particular Opportunity Zones in areas with higher incomes, growing economies, and thriving real estate markets. While many communities such as Fall River, Springfield, and New Bedford could benefit from additional economic incentives, other areas like tourist-heavy Provincetown and dynamic Somerville are already experiencing economic booms. As such, both policymakers and investors should be aware of the large variation across designated Opportunity Zones. Some communities are already rapidly developing and threfore pose less invetment risk, while others may be in greater need of investment and therefore pose more investment risk. Responsible policy and investment requires being aware of these dynamics.

Housing Indicators

Indicators relating to rent can provide information on the fragility of the current housing market and whether greater development will result in economic gains for the community or gentrification. Housing development statistics indicate whether a community is already experiencing a building boom.

Economic Indicators

Indicators relating to income, inequality, and employment provide important information about the current economic health of a community. This can provide guidance on where additional investment may be most needed.
